  • Workout Plans

  • Monday (Chest Day):

    • Bench Press: 4 sets of 8-10 reps
      • Warm-up set: 12 reps with lighter weight
      • Increase weight gradually for each working set
    • Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Adjust bench to a 30-45 degree incline
      • Use challenging weight but maintain proper form
    • Chest Flyes: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
      • Use dumbbells or cables for flye movements
      • Focus on the stretch and contraction of the chest muscles
    • Push-Ups: 3 sets to failure
      • Perform as many reps as possible with good form
      • Modify intensity by changing hand placement or elevating feet
    • Dips: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
      • Use parallel bars or assisted dip machine if needed
      • Lower body until upper arms are parallel to the ground

  • Tuesday (Leg Day):

    • Squats: 4 sets of 8-10 reps
      • Warm-up with bodyweight or light weight first
      • Focus on proper squat form, including depth and knee alignment
    • Leg Press: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Adjust seat position for comfort and range of motion
      • Keep feet shoulder-width apart on the platform
    • Lunges: 3 sets of 12 steps per leg
      • Use dumbbells for added resistance if desired
      • Keep front knee behind toes during lunging motion
    • Leg Extensions: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
      • Adjust seat and weight stack for proper form
      • Squeeze quads at the top of each rep
    • Leg Curls: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Focus on controlled movement and full range of motion
      • Adjust machine settings for comfort and intensity
    • Calf Raises: 3 sets of 15-20 reps
      • Use a calf raise machine or a platform for standing calf raises
      • Lower heels below the level of the platform for a full stretch

  • Wednesday (Back Day):

    • Deadlifts: 4 sets of 6-8 reps
      • Warm-up with lighter weights and practice form
      • Use a mixed grip for heavier sets if needed
    • Pull-Ups/Chin-Ups: 3 sets to failure
      • Use wide grip for pull-ups and close grip for chin-ups
      • Use assistance or bands if unable to complete reps unassisted
    • Barbell Rows: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
      • Keep back flat and core engaged throughout the movement
      • Use an overhand grip with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width
    • Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Use a wide grip for targeting the lats
      • Avoid swinging or using momentum to lift the weight
    • T-Bar Rows: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Use a T-bar row machine or landmine attachment
      • Keep back straight and pull towards the hips

  • Thursday (Shoulder Day):

    • Military Press/Overhead Press: 4 sets of 8-10 reps
      • Use a barbell or dumbbells for overhead pressing
      • Keep core tight and avoid arching the back excessively
    • Side Lateral Raises: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
      • Use dumbbells and maintain a slight bend in the elbows
      • Lift arms to shoulder height with palms facing down
    • Front Raises: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Use dumbbells or a barbell for front deltoid targeting
      • Keep arms straight with a slight bend at the elbows
    • Rear Delt Flyes: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
      • Use cables, dumbbells, or a rear delt machine
      • Focus on squeezing the rear delts at the top of each rep
    • Shrugs: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
      • Use dumbbells or a barbell for shrugging motion
      • Elevate shoulders towards ears and hold for a moment at the top

  • Friday (Arms Day):

    • Bicep Curls (Barbell/Dumbbell): 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Use a variety of grips (wide, narrow, hammer) for variation
      • Control the movement and avoid swinging the weight
    • Tricep Dips/Extensions: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Use dip bars or a tricep extension machine
      • Lower body until elbows are at 90 degrees or slightly below
    • Hammer Curls: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Keep palms facing each other throughout the curl
      • Focus on squeezing the biceps at the top of the movement
    • Tricep Pushdowns: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
      • Use a cable machine with a straight or V-bar attachment
      • Keep elbows close to the body and extend arms fully
    • Preacher Curls: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Use a preacher curl bench or a preacher curl machine
      • Maintain strict form and focus on the bicep contraction
    • Skull Crushers: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
      • Use an EZ curl bar or dumbbells for skull crushers
      • Lower weight towards forehead while keeping elbows stationary

  • Saturday (Cardio):

    • Squats: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
    • Use a weight that challenges you but allows for proper form.
    • Keep your back straight and lower your hips until thighs are parallel to the ground.
    • Push-ups: 3 sets to failure or modified push-ups if needed
    • Focus on full range of motion and keep core engaged.
    • Modify by doing knee push-ups or using an incline surface.
    • Bent-over Rows: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
    • Use a barbell or dumbbells and maintain a flat back throughout the movement.
    • Pull the weight towards your lower chest, squeezing your shoulder blades.
    • Plank: 3 sets of 30-60 seconds
    • Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    • Engage your core muscles and avoid sagging or arching your back.
